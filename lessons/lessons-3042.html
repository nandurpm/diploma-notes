Mini Formula Bank

Mini Formula Bank - Principles of Electronic Communication

Quick reference cards for revision. Use this only after reading the module explanation.

AM modulation index

m = Vm/Vc

Meaning: Ratio of modulating amplitude to carrier amplitude.

Where used: AM numerical problems.

Common mistake: Allowing m > 1 without mentioning over-modulation.

AM bandwidth

BW = 2fm

Meaning: Bandwidth for single tone AM.

Where used: AM spectrum problems.

Common mistake: Using carrier frequency as bandwidth.

AM total power

Pt = Pc(1 + m²/2)

Meaning: Power in carrier plus sidebands.

Where used: AM power calculations.

Common mistake: Using m in percent without dividing by 100.

FM modulation index

β = Δf/fm

Meaning: Ratio of frequency deviation to modulating frequency.

Where used: FM analysis.

Common mistake: Confusing Δf with carrier frequency.

Carson rule

BW ≈ 2(Δf + fm)

Meaning: Approximate FM bandwidth.

Where used: FM bandwidth calculation.

Common mistake: Using AM bandwidth formula for FM.

Sampling theorem

fs ≥ 2fm

Meaning: Sampling frequency must be at least twice maximum signal frequency.

Where used: PAM/PCM basics.

Common mistake: Sampling below Nyquist rate.

AM detector index

m = (Vmax-Vmin)/(Vmax+Vmin)

Meaning: Envelope method for AM modulation index.

Where used: Lab/receiver demodulation.

Common mistake: Measuring envelope incorrectly.
