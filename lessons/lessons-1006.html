Mini Formula Bank

Important formulas used in Applied Physics Lab

Use the correct unit and write the final result with proper significant figures.

Vernier Least Count

LC = 1 MSD - 1 VSD

Used for accurate length measurement. Common mistake: forgetting zero correction.

Screw Gauge Least Count

LC = pitch / number of head scale divisions

Used for diameter or thickness. Apply positive/negative zero correction carefully.

Hooke's Law

F = kx

Force is proportional to extension within elastic limit. Unit of k is N m⁻¹.

Ohm's Law

V = IR

From V-I graph, resistance is slope V/I. Keep connections tight.

Simple Pendulum

g = 4π²l / T²

Length is measured from point of suspension to centre of bob.

Lens Power

P = 1 / f

Use focal length in metre. Unit of power is dioptre.

Snell's Law

sin i / sin r = constant

Used in glass slab or prism refraction experiment.

Series / Parallel Resistance

Rs = R1 + R2, 1/Rp = 1/R1 + 1/R2

Used to verify laws of resistance combinations.

Exam Writing Tips

  • For graph experiments, mark axes with quantity and unit.
  • Write slope method clearly when result depends on graph.
  • In pendulum experiment, take time for multiple oscillations to reduce error.
  • Never overwrite observations; rewrite neatly if a reading is wrong.

Module 3 • CO3 • 6 hours

Ray optics experiments

Use lens, glass slab and prism experiments to understand focal length, power and refraction.

Convex Lens by u-v Method

Measure object distance u and image distance v for different positions. Use lens formula or graph method to determine focal length and power.

  • Image must be sharp and inverted on screen.
  • Distances are measured from optical centre.
  • Power is calculated using focal length in metre.

Snell's Law

Verify law of refraction using a glass slab or prism by measuring angle of incidence and refraction. The ratio sin i / sin r remains constant for a pair of media.

Master Answer Key

Answers for expected questions

Each answer matches the module question numbering.

M1-Q1

Vernier calipers measure small lengths accurately using main scale and vernier scale. Least count is the difference between one main scale division and one vernier scale division. Corrected reading = observed reading ± zero correction.

M1-Q2

In screw gauge, positive zero error is subtracted and negative zero error is added. Final diameter = main scale reading + head scale coincidence × least count ± correction.

M1-Q3

Spherometer record includes aim, apparatus, least count, distance between legs, central screw readings on plane and curved surface, sagitta, calculation and radius of curvature.

M1-Q4

Read the thermometer at eye level, avoid touching bulb with vessel wall, wait until mercury level becomes steady and handle mercury thermometer safely.

M2-Q1

Hooke's law states that extension is directly proportional to applied force within elastic limit. Plot load versus extension; spring constant k = force / extension or slope of F-x graph.

M2-Q2

Terminal velocity is the constant velocity reached when viscous force, buoyancy and weight are balanced. It is needed in Stoke's law to calculate coefficient of viscosity.

M2-Q3

Moment bar works on principle of moments: in equilibrium, clockwise moment equals anticlockwise moment. Unknown mass is found by balancing known mass at measured distances.

M2-Q4

For a simple pendulum, measure length l and time period T. Use g = 4π²l/T². Take time for many oscillations and calculate mean T for accuracy.

M2-Q5

Keep tuning fork just above resonance tube, strike gently, avoid touching prongs, note water level accurately and apply end correction where required.

M3-Q1

Aim: determine focal length of convex lens. Measure u and v for sharp images, use 1/f = 1/u + 1/v or graph method, calculate f and power.

M3-Q2

Power of a lens is reciprocal of focal length in metre. P = 1/f. Unit is dioptre.

M3-Q3

Snell's law: sin i / sin r is constant for a given pair of media. Observation table contains angle of incidence, angle of refraction, sin i, sin r and ratio.

M3-Q4

Draw incident ray, normal, refracted ray inside glass slab and emergent ray. Label i, r, e, lateral displacement and parallel faces of slab.

M4-Q1

Connect ammeter in series and voltmeter in parallel with resistor. Vary voltage, note V and I, plot V against I. Straight line through origin verifies Ohm's law; slope gives resistance.

M4-Q2

For series combination, Rs = R1 + R2 + ... . For parallel combination, 1/Rp = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ... . Verify by comparing calculated and measured equivalent resistance.

M4-Q3

Knee voltage is the forward voltage at which diode current starts increasing rapidly. It is approximately 0.7 V for silicon and 0.3 V for germanium diode.

M4-Q4

Potentiometer works on potential gradient. At balance point, no current flows through galvanometer and EMF is proportional to balancing length.

M4-Q5

In meter bridge, connect unknown resistance and known resistance in two gaps, find balance point, calculate unknown resistance using bridge balance condition and then find resistivity if wire dimensions are given.
