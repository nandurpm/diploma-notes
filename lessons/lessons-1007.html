Mini Formula Bank

Mini Formula Bank for Applied Chemistry Lab

Use this as the last-minute memory page before exams and practical sessions.

Molarity

M = moles / litre

Where used: Solution preparation

Common mistake: Volume must be in litre.

Normality equation

N₁V₁ = N₂V₂

Where used: Titration

Common mistake: Use concordant titre value.

Strength

Strength = Normality × Equivalent mass

Where used: Solution analysis

Common mistake: Units: g/L.

pH

pH = −log[H⁺]

Where used: Acid/base measurement

Common mistake: Lower pH means higher acidity.

Hardness

Expressed as ppm CaCO₃

Where used: Water analysis

Common mistake: Do not omit unit.

Faraday law

m = ZIt

Where used: Electrolysis

Common mistake: Time in seconds.

Important definitions / formulas / key points

  • Molarity: M = moles / litre — Solution preparation
  • Normality equation: N₁V₁ = N₂V₂ — Titration
  • Strength: Strength = Normality × Equivalent mass — Solution analysis
  • pH: pH = −log[H⁺] — Acid/base measurement
  • Hardness: Expressed as ppm CaCO₃ — Water analysis
  • Faraday law: m = ZIt — Electrolysis

  • Forgetting diagrams, tables, units, procedures or examples.
